Introduction to Tersive Shorthand

Tersive is a modern shorthand writing system. It comes in two flavors: one for keyboards (typing on a computer or typewriter), and one for handwritten notes and dictation. Tersive is logical and easy to learn quickly so you can begin using it right away. But in order to become really efficient, as with any skill, it will take a great deal of practice.

Features

  • Tersive has two flavors: Script and Key. Tersive Script is for handwriting, Tersive Key is for keyboarding (typing).
  • You do not write letters or spell words. You write or type phonemes (distinct speech sounds). You only need to know how a word sounds in order to write it in Tersive.
  • Vowels, such as schwa, a few consonants, and parts of words and even phrases, may be omitted when the word or phrase is unlikely to be misinterpreted. A rule of thumb is: if you don't actually pronounce a phoneme (speech sound) when speaking quickly, it's safe to omit from your writing.
  • Tersive has 4 levels of mastery. The greater the level the higher the efficiency and speed, but the more effort is required to master.

Tersive Key Features

  • Very easy to learn and start using right away.
  • Each key represents a specific phoneme (sound). In order to represent all of the possible phonemes some capital letters are needed, as well as a few other keys.
  • Most diphthongs (combined vowels, such as 'ei' as in eight) also have assigned keys in order to speed up your writing.
  • Words are keyed with a space between each. There are also common phrases which are keyed together without spacing within the phrase.
  • We omit all punctuation marks except period and question mark.

Tersive Script Features

  • Glyphs (strokes) are connected for words and separated with a small space between each word, and written horizontally. Note, there are common phrases which are also connected.
  • Some consonants have a truncated shape at the beginning or end of a sequence for efficiency.
  • Glyphs are usually written along the line using lined writing paper, especially for beginners.
  • Phonemes which sound similar have similar glyphs.
  • There are as many glyphs written above the line as below.
  • Tersive Script has only two punctuation marks, one for period and one for question mark.
  • Most diphthongs (combined vowels, such as 'ei' as in eight) have special glyphs in order to speed up your writing.

Learning

Tersive is learned at multiple levels or stages. At level 1 you simply write everything phonetically. The speed increase for writing this way is minor for typing but actually significant for scripting. Level 2 lets you drop or omit many of the less important parts of words. Thus both your typing and scripting speed should increase significantly. Level 3 is mostly for scripting, where you learn to simplify your strokes, especially vowels. Key users learn to stop using shifted (capital) letters. Your speed should increase by a major factor at this level. But the most significant speed will be gained at level 4. By removing all redundancies, we learn to write many common words and phrases much simpler than previous levels. You can expect dramatic improvement at this level. But level 4 requires the most effort and memorization.

Tersive Shorthand is a modern technique of either handwriting or typing (keyboarding) English quickly using simplified, contiguous strokes (glyphs) or fewer keys. Like other shorthand systems, the glyphs or keys represent spoken sounds (phonemes), not letters. Both modes of writing have many commonalities allowing many things you learn in one to be used for the other.

 

For handwriting, Tersive Shorthand is most often written along lined paper, both above and below the line. Except for numbers, consonants and vowels glyphs start from or end at the line, often both. Vowels are loops, except for the schwa, which is a short dash on the line.

 

For keyboarding, we use the qwerty keyboard letters provided, usually individually, to represent phonemes. Note, you must use a simple text editor or turn off automatic spelling correction in a word processor or you will have problems.

 

There are 4 levels of Tersive expertise, but can be used at any level. Each level builds on previous ones. The levels are gradually more difficult to learn (with more to memorize) but provide more shortcuts, which enable faster writing.

 

This app can work in either Hand or Type mode, as a tool to help you learn. It is focused on Level 4, which takes the most time and practice to learn. But for the best experience we strongly recommend that you also get the Tersive Shorthand Book.
